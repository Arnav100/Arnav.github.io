About Me

Hi, I am Arnav Parashar, a student at Nichols School class of '20. I love learning and applying various areas of computer programming. I created this website as both an exercise in my pursuit of programming knowledge and to document what I have learned for the use of others. As much as I've come to love learning the subject, over this past year, I've also realized I enjoy teaching programming just as much. I am the programming captain in my school's FRC team, Electric Mayhem 4930, and at the start of the season, I created a mini curriculum to teach our six new programmers java and how to program a robot, resulting in each of them being able to help program our 2019 FRC Robot. Since then, I wanted to continue teaching others and have worked a part-time summer job at a center called Robothink where I teach how to build and program small robots to young children.

On this website, I plan to write up a guide on how my team programs an FRC robot, to leave as a resource for future 4930 programmers and others within FRC. I also aim to document the work done on my senior thesis, a lidar controlled robot, along with some of my past projects like the 2019 FRC scouting app I created for my team and my team's website/blog. If this website can help anyone with an issue they have or inspire them to take on a similar project to mine, that would be awesome. I am nowhere near an expert on programming and this website is really just me documenting my work and thought process as I learn new things, so of course there may be a better solution to what I come up with in my projects ( And if you'd like to inform me, I'd love to know about it). Feel free to ask questions, offer advice, or ask me to work on a project for you.
